## The Core Thesis

Voice agents are not new.

What’s new is that:
- They finally work
- They are cheap enough
- They can be deployed without specialized teams

This creates a rare **application-layer opportunity**.

---

## Why Voice Failed Before

Historically:
- Speech recognition was brittle
- Latency was unacceptable
- Systems were scripted and fragile
- Deployment required custom engineering

Voice IVRs were hated — not adopted.

---

## What Changed

### 1. Realtime Speech-to-Speech

- Natural turn-taking
- Interruptions feel human
- Conversations no longer feel robotic

---

### 2. General-Purpose Reasoning Models

- Can interpret intent, not just keywords
- Handle ambiguity and corrections
- Adapt language naturally

---

### 3. Infrastructure Commoditization

- Multiple interchangeable providers
- Falling costs
- No lock-in required

This shifts value **up the stack**.

---

## Why Applications Win Now

Customers don’t want:
- APIs
- Prompts
- Agent builders

They want:
- Phones answered
- Appointments booked
- Leads captured
- Guests taken care of

This is an application problem, not a tooling problem.

---

## Why These Verticals First

The chosen verticals share traits:
- Phone is already the primary UI
- Missed calls = lost revenue
- Labor is expensive and scarce
- Workflows are repetitive but high-stakes

Voice agents slot in naturally.

---

## Market Timing

- Millions of businesses already pay for phone handling
- They are actively seeking automation
- Early deployments show clear ROI
- Buyers are no longer skeptical — they’re impatient

---

## Competitive Landscape

Most competitors:
- Are horizontal platforms
- Sell tools, not outcomes
- Require configuration and tuning

This product:
- Ships opinionated workflows
- Is vertical-specific
- Is outcome-driven

---

## The Bet

The bet is not on:
- A specific LLM
- A specific TTS engine

The bet is on:
- Owning high-value workflows
- Becoming infrastructure for operations
- Being extremely hard to replace once embedded

---

## Summary

This is a classic platform shift:
- Infrastructure becomes cheap
- Applications become valuable
- Distribution and workflow depth decide winners

Voice is the UI.
Workflows are the moat.
Now is the moment.
