## Overview

This document defines and sizes the **U.S. Serviceable Addressable Market (SAM)** for **voice agent applications** across five target verticals:

1. Luxury Boutique Hotels (including micro resorts)
2. Healthcare Access & Scheduling
3. Assisted Living & Home Care
4. Home Services (HVAC, plumbing, electrical, pest control)
5. Auto Services (repair, maintenance, oil, tires)

The focus is explicitly on **application-layer voice agents**:
- Products where **voice is the primary UI**
- Built on top of commoditized infrastructure (e.g. OpenAI Realtime API, ElevenLabs, telephony APIs)
- Sold as **outcome-driven software**, not compute or minutes

---

## What Counts as the Market

### Included
- Packaged voice applications sold to businesses
- Voice-first workflows (phone is the primary channel)
- Subscription, per-location, per-booking, or revenue-share pricing
- Automation of real operational work (scheduling, intake, dispatch, concierge, coordination)

### Excluded
- Infrastructure providers (LLMs, TTS, STT, telephony)
- Horizontal agent-building platforms
- Professional services or one-off integrations
- Chat-only conversational AI

## Segment 1: Luxury Boutique Hotels (Including Micro Resorts)

### Definition

Luxury boutique hotels are:
- Independent or small-brand properties
- Typically 20–100 rooms (micro resorts may be smaller)
- High ADR and experience-driven
- Concierge-heavy operations

Micro resorts (e.g. Live Oak Lake) fall within this segment and are treated as **a subcategory**, not a standalone market.

### Market Size (U.S.)

- Boutique hotel industry revenue (U.S.): ~$36.5B annually
- Number of boutique hotel properties: ~6,000+
- Luxury segment represents the highest ADR and service intensity

### Voice-Relevant Spend

Hotels rely heavily on:
- Phone reservations and modifications
- Concierge requests and coordination
- Vendor bookings (transport, dining, tours)
- Guest services and internal ops routing

### U.S. SAM (Voice Applications)

**Conservative estimate:**  
- 10–30% of properties adopt voice automation
- $200–$1,000 per property per month equivalent value

**Resulting U.S. SAM:**  
**$500M – $2B annually**

## Segment 2: Healthcare Access & Scheduling

### Target Subsegments

- Physical therapy clinics
- Dental offices
- Specialty medical practices

### Problem Characteristics

- Extremely high inbound call volume
- Scheduling, rescheduling, cancellations
- Insurance and intake questions
- Chronic no-shows
- Staff burnout from phone handling

Voice is already the default interface for patients.

### Market Indicators (U.S.)

- ~140,000 dental offices
- ~5,000+ physical therapy clinics
- Tens of thousands of specialty practices
- Millions of inbound scheduling calls daily

### U.S. SAM (Voice Applications)

**Assumptions**
- Per-location pricing or per-booked-appointment fees
- Clear ROI via reduced no-shows and staff hours saved

**Resulting U.S. SAM:**  
**$800M – $3B annually**

## Voice Agent Application: Core Feature Spec

### 1. Intake & Triage
- Natural inbound voice conversations
- Intent detection (schedule, cancel, request, status)
- Structured data capture
- Intelligent escalation to humans

### 2. Orchestration
- Availability checks
- Calendar and system integrations
- Multi-step workflow execution
- Vendor or staff coordination

### 3. Confirmations & Follow-Up
- Voice + SMS confirmations
- Reminders
- Two-way updates
- No-show reduction flows

### 4. Integration Layer
- Calendars
- PMS / EMR / Dispatch systems
- CRM and billing tools

### 5. Admin & Analytics
- Call analytics
- Conversion metrics
- Override and review tools
- SLA and escalation rules

### 6. Compliance & Trust
- HIPAA (healthcare)
- Secure data handling
- Call recording and retention controls
